## Advanced Tax Strategies for 2026

### Appreciated Assets: The Utah Advantage

**Common scenarios:**
- Investment property that's tripled in value
- Company stock with large unrealized gains
- Business sale creating substantial capital gains
- Inherited assets with step-up basis opportunities

### Installment Sales Strategy

**How it works:** Structure asset sales to spread proceeds over multiple years instead of taking one large lump sum

**Utah benefits:**
- Lower state income tax on capital gains
- Ability to stay in moderate tax brackets
- Coordination with retirement account withdrawals
- Estate planning flexibility

**Real example:** Utah client sold rental property using installment sale, saved enough in taxes to buy a new truck outright.

### Charitable Remainder Trusts (CRTs)

**Perfect for charitable Utah retirees:**
- Transfer appreciated assets to trust before sale
- No immediate tax on sale within trust
- Receive lifetime income payments
- Immediate charitable tax deduction
- Remainder goes to chosen Utah charity

**Case study:** Client with $500,000 appreciated stock, original basis $100,000:
- Traditional sale: $400,000 taxable gain
- CRT strategy: No immediate tax, full $500,000 invested for income
- Annual income stream for life
- Significant charitable deduction
- Legacy to local Utah organization

## Estate Planning Meets Tax Strategy

### Utah's Estate Planning Advantages

**No state estate tax:** Simplifies planning compared to states with additional estate taxes
**Efficient probate process:** Lower costs and faster resolution than many states
**Family-friendly laws:** Support for caregiving and multi-generational planning

### The $10 Million Opportunity

Section 1202 small business stock can allow up to $10 million in capital gains exclusion—not $1 million, but $10 million.

**Utah entrepreneurs benefit:** Local business owners selling their companies can potentially eliminate massive tax obligations through proper structure.

### IRA/401(k) Tax Time Bombs

**The problem:** Large retirement accounts create multiple tax hits for heirs:
- Income tax on withdrawals (highest tax rates)
- Potential estate tax on large balances
- 10-year withdrawal requirement (SECURE Act)
- 25% penalty for missed distributions

**Utah solutions:**
- Strategic Roth conversions during lifetime
- Charitable distributions to reduce taxable balance
- Family education about inheritance tax implications
- Professional coordination between generations

### Question: Emergency Fund vs. Retirement Savings

**From Bryce:** "I'm 56 and want $1 million saved before I retire. I'm doing great with my 401(k) but can't seem to save much cash. How do I build an emergency fund?"

**Mike's advice:** "Your emergency fund is like a financial seatbelt—you hope you never need it, but when life slams on the brakes, it keeps you from flying through the windshield financially."

**Building strategy:**
- **Automate it:** Treat emergency fund like a bill you owe yourself
- **Separate account:** Hard to reach, not attached to debit card
- **Make it a game:** Save every $5 bill, throw found money into fund
- **Remember Benjamin Franklin:** "Beware of little expenses; a small leak will sink a great ship"

**The result:** You'll sleep better knowing you have a cushion for life's unexpected moments.
