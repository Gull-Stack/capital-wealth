## January: New Year, New Financial Foundation

### Priority Actions for Utah Retirees

**Tax Document Organization**
- Gather all 2025 tax documents (1099s, 1098s, K-1s)
- Review Utah state tax requirements and advantages
- Calculate estimated Social Security tax savings (Utah doesn't tax benefits)
- Plan Roth conversion opportunities for the year

**Required Minimum Distribution (RMD) Planning**
- Calculate 2026 RMD requirements for IRA and 401(k) accounts
- Consider timing distributions to manage tax brackets
- Explore Qualified Charitable Distributions (QCDs) to Utah charities
- Review beneficiary designations on all retirement accounts

**Insurance Review**
- Evaluate Medicare Supplement insurance options
- Review long-term care insurance policies
- Assess life insurance needs for estate planning
- Verify coverage with Utah healthcare providers

### Utah-Specific January Opportunities

**Municipal Bond Review:** Research Utah municipal bonds for tax-free income opportunities
**Healthcare Provider Network:** Confirm all doctors and specialists remain in your Medicare network
**Property Tax Planning:** Review property tax assessments and appeal deadlines if necessary

### Real Utah Client Example: The Hendersons from Murray

The Hendersons, ages 68 and 65, used January to reorganize their entire tax strategy. By planning strategic Roth conversions throughout 2026, they projected saving $34,000 in lifetime taxes while maintaining their desired income level.
